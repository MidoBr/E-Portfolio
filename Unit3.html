Research Proposal Review: Cloud computing in building effective healthcare information system

• Which of the methods described in this week's reading would you think would suit your purpose?


According to George, 2021 exploratory research methods allow researchers to explore and investigate particular areas of the topic in depth. Irshad et al. (2015) performed an exploratory analysis to show that cloud computing technologies offer different critical services that include computing, networking, storage, and databases, thus exploratory methods will enable researcher to:
  • Stay up to date with the latest developments and identify emerging trends in cloud computing.
  • Explore different angles, perspectives, and possibilities, which can be useful for innovation and problem-solving.
  • Identify gaps in existing knowledge and research related to cloud computing.
  • Gather preliminary insights and gather initial data about cloud computing.
  • Exploring user experiences and perceptions: Cloud computing involves the interaction of users with various services and technologies. By using exploratory research methods such as interviews, surveys, or observations, you can explore user experiences, concerns, and perceptions related to cloud computing.
  • Identify potential challenges, risks, and opportunities associated with cloud computing.
  • Exploratory research often serves as a foundation for more focused and rigorous studies in the future.

• Which data collection methods would you consider using?


Providing a comprehensive understanding of how cloud computing technologies contribute to building effective healthcare information systems require different methods of data collection, Cote (2021) of Harvard business school explained these methods as following:

  • Surveys: Surveys are a common quantitative data collection method that can be used to gather large-scale data from cloud computing users or professionals. Surveys can help collect structured data on various aspects such as user satisfaction, usage patterns, performance metrics, or security concerns. The survey results can provide quantitative insights and help identify trends or patterns.
  • Interviews: Interviews are a qualitative data collection method that allows researchers to gather in-depth information and insights from individuals with expertise in cloud computing.
  • Case studies: Case studies involve in-depth investigation of a particular cloud computing implementation, organization, or project. They combine qualitative and quantitative data collection methods, such as interviews, observations, document analysis, and performance metrics.
  • Focus groups: Focus groups bring together a small group of participants who engage in facilitated discussions about cloud computing. This method allows researchers to gather qualitative data on participants' opinions, experiences, and perceptions in a group setting.
