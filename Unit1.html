Initial Post

In a world that is constantly evolving due to technological advancements, the ACM code of ethics plays a crucial role in promoting ethical conduct and behavior among technology professionals and organizations. A case study involving Rouge Services company, which pledged to deliver its web hosting services to its customers 'no matter what', highlights the importance of the code of ethics in remediating such violations. The company's malware disruption resulted in the hosting of spam services and adware-related codes on Rouge's servers among other violations, making it necessary to apply the code of ethics to help remediate the violations (ACM, 2018).

According to Lukings & Lashkari, 2022 “Crimes that can be committed with or without the use of technology, but are amplified in their scope or impact through the utilization of computers, computer networks, and other technological means are referred to as cyber-enabled crimes.” In this case study Rouge services is playing the role of enabler of cybercrimes. The pledge made by Rouge services enables cyber-criminals to maintain robust resources to commit their crimes and yet stay online.

On other hand, considering the potential negative consequences that might affect the social aspect of the public good, Rouge Services is providing the right atmosphere for malicious actors to manipulate individuals using several approaches such as social engineering and fraudulent activities. This in turn, could lead to a breach of privacy and rising security concerns in society (Carley, 2020).

The professionalism of computing professionals involved in malware disruption is essential to effectively combatting the negative effects of malware attacks. These professionals are dedicated to identifying, analyzing, and mitigating the damage caused by malware, while also taking steps to prevent future attacks by applying their ethics, expertise, and collaboration. (BCS, 2022).

Rouge Services has violated some of the guidelines set by the BCS. The hosting services provided by Rouge Services enable malicious individuals or firms to host harmful codes or tools that could potentially harm the public, which is a violation of principle 1.a of the BCS code of conduct (BCS, 2022).
