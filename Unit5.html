Well Fargo's Cross-Selling Scandal

The Wells Fargo cross-selling scandal was a significant blow to the company's reputation, which had previously been considered one of the most respected companies in the United States. The aggressive tactics used by some employees - who already have access to customers data - to meet their targets not only violated the trust of customers but also resulted in regulatory fines and investigations. This scandal highlighted the importance of maintaining ethical practices and corporate responsibility, especially for large companies with a considerable impact on the financial industry. While Wells Fargo has taken steps to address the issue and rebuild trust with its customers, the incident serves as a cautionary tale of the potential consequences of prioritizing sales targets over ethical business practices (Tayan, 2019). It is essential for companies to prioritize transparency, accountability, and ethical behavior to maintain the trust of their customers and the public.

The failing of addressing the root cause of the violations of ethical and professional practices are derived from the prevalent culture among Wells Fargo staff. The avoidable profit-oriented management motivated employees to be incompetent, negligent and follow whatever possible means to meet specified targets to cover the inadequate incentive they are receiving (Massawe, 2020).

Reducing the social impact and the public opinion of the scandal, requires Wells Fargo to address some key areas that played magnificent role in the way employees behave. In 2017 Wells Fargo announced new incentives approach that focused on the level of customer satisfaction and eliminated the product sales goals to promote more ethical behavior by the employees (Witman, 2018).
